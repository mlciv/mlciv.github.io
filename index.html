    I am a post-doctoral researcher at NSF AI Institute for Student-AI Teaming (iSAT) where I mainly work with James Martin, Martha Palmer, and Boulder NLP Group Before this, I obtained my Ph.D. from the Kahlert School of Computing at the University of Utah, where I worked with Vivek Srikumar and the Uath NLP Group. Building systems capable of understanding and responding in a conversational context is a crucial frontier of AI technology. My overarching goal is to facilitate domain-specific conversational modeling via natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning (ML) approaches. My research addresses the following challenges:

    • Modularized NLP: Structured Prediction and Controllable Generation. To ensure that robustness is not lost when embracing black-box neural advances, domain-specific conversational systems could benefit from adhering to interpretable modularized designs, by dividing tasks into logically abstracted or theory-grounded sub-goals according to our desired controllability. My research studies deep structured prediction for extracting symbolic representations from text ([Dissertation'22] , [CoNLL'19], [ACL'19],[NAACL'21] ,[IWSDS'23]) and generating text with constraints ([DSTC7'19],[ACL'19], [BEA'23]).
    • Learning with Inductive Biases. Beyond expensive supervised data, we investigate domain-specific knowledge as inductive biases (e.g., psychotherapy, teaching, tutoring, small-group collaboration), such as linguistic knowledge about lexical and phrasal anchoring [CoNLL'19], database qeury structures to guide self-supervised learning [VLDB'22], description-driven dialogue and supplementary pretaining [NAACL'21].
    • Deployment in Real-world Scenarios.Unexpected, complex dialogue scenarios can potentially span infinitely many topics, states and envrionmental settings [ACL'23], causing severe robustness issues for our AI partner design [AIAIC'23], such as noisy speech[UMAP'23], multi-party [IWSDS'23] and multi-modal dynamics.
