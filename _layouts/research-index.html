I am a post-doctoral researcher at NSF AI Institute for Student-AI Teaming (iSAT) where I mainly work with James Martin, Martha Palmer, and Boulder NLP Group Before this, I obtained my Ph.D. from the Kahlert School of Computing at the University of Utah, where I worked with Vivek Srikumar and the Uath NLP Group. Building systems capable of understanding and responding in a conversational context is a crucial frontier of AI technology. My overarching goal is to facilitate domain-specific conversational modeling via natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning (ML) approaches. My research addresses the following challenges:

  • Modularized NLP: Structured Prediction and Controllable Generation. To ensure that robustness is not lost when embracing neural advances, domain-specific conversational systems could benefit from adhering to interpretable modularized designs by dividing tasks into logically abstracted or theory-grounded sub-goals according to our desired controllability. My research studies deep structured prediction for extracting symbolic representations from text and generating text with constraints.
  • Learning with Less Supervision. We investigate domain-specific~(e.g., psychotherapy, teaching, tutoring, small-group collaboration) learning approaches with self-supervised learning, description-driven dialogue, and supplementary pretaining.
  • Deployment in Real-world Scenarios.Unexpected, complex dynamic dialogue scenarios can potentially span infinitely many topics, states and envrionmental settings, causing severe robustness issues for our AI partner design, such as noisy speech, multi-party and multi-modal dynamics.
